Reporting Guidelines

Recommendations from our database analysis to improve comparability and deployment-relevance of DAC sorbent studies.

When CO₂ capacity is plotted for all sorbents under all conditions, every class appears to show a positive trend. Once only sorbents tested under standard conditions are isolated, only amine sorbents show a statistically meaningful positive trend — MOF and metal oxide sorbents have too few entries to analyze.

Standard conditions were identified as the most frequently reported (mode) value for each parameter within each sorbent class. The percentage in parentheses is the fraction of that class tested under that condition.

Applying the standard conditions sequentially reduces 914 sorbents to just 335 (37%). For amine sorbents: 312 of 710 (44%). For MOFs: only 4 of 42 (10%). For metal oxides: 15 of 56 (27%), but zero have sufficient breakthrough data to calculate cycle time.

Recommendation: Adopt standard conditions analogous to NREL's photovoltaic testing program. Always report all experimental conditions — even small differences substantially alter measured performance values.

Only about 8% of sorbents in the database have been characterized under all four deployment-relevant conditions: atmospheric CO₂ concentrations, oxidative environments, humid conditions, and breakthrough measurements.

Relative Humidity

At moderate humidity (20–60% RH), water can increase CO₂ capacity of amine sorbents by shifting the reaction mechanism toward ammonium bicarbonate. At high humidity, excess water loading raises regeneration energy and blocks binding sites.

Ambient Temperature

Lower temperatures increase adsorption affinity but raise desorption energy. DAC sites range from hot-dry desert to cold subarctic conditions.

Altitude and CO₂ Partial Pressure

At 2,000 m elevation, CO₂ partial pressure is ~0.34 mbar vs. ~0.42 mbar at sea level. We recommend reporting CO₂ isotherms across 0.35–0.45 mbar.

Oxygen Exposure

DAC operates in ~21% O₂. Oxygen at elevated regeneration temperatures causes oxidative amine degradation. Stability testing must include simulated air conditions.

Varying working capacity, cycle time, regeneration energy, and sorbent lifetime by ±30% can shift the modeled cost of CO₂ removal by hundreds of dollars per ton.

Data collection
Recorded Parameters

18 parameters were recorded for each entry. Text and numerical values were extracted from each paper; graphical data was digitized using WebPlotDigitizer. Values not reported are marked nr; not-applicable values are marked na.

Year

Year of publication of the source paper.

Sorbent Class

Amine (any amine functional group interacting with CO₂), MOF (without amine CO₂-binding), Metal oxide (inorganic metal–oxygen compounds), or Other (COFs, zeolites, ionic liquids, etc.).

Material Abbreviation

Short identifier from the source paper. Additional descriptors added where necessary to distinguish similar materials.

CO₂ Adsorption Capacity

CO₂ uptake in mmol per gram of sorbent, converted from whatever units were reported. For isotherms, the value at ~400 ppm CO₂.

Capture Temperature

Temperature (°C) during CO₂ adsorption. "Room temperature" entries recorded as 25 °C.

Release Temperature

Temperature (°C) during regeneration. For vacuum-swing materials, the temperature at which vacuum was applied.

CO₂ Concentration

CO₂ concentration (ppm) in the feed gas. "Ambient air" entries recorded as 425 ppm. Only sorbents below 5,000 ppm were included.

Capture Gas

Y = Air (O₂ present during adsorption); N = Inert + CO₂ (no oxygen). Oxygen can drive oxidative amine degradation.

Regeneration Gas

Gas used during desorption. Common values: N₂, He, Ar, vacuum, or mixtures with CO₂ or H₂O.

Relative Humidity

RH (%) of the capture feed gas. Also converted to mole percent and dew point (°C). 0% = dry conditions.

Number of Cycles

Total adsorption–desorption cycles tested. Zero = single adsorption. ~20% of sorbents report any cycling data.

Degradation Rate (%/cycle)

Average percent capacity loss per cycle: [(q₁ − q_last) / q₁] × 100 / N. Only sorbents with ≥2 cycles and first/last capacities reported.

Degradation Cause

Mechanism of degradation as reported. Categories: Thermal, Oxidation, Hydrolysis, CO₂-catalyzed, Pore blockage, Structural, Negligible, No mechanism suggested, and combinations.

CO₂ Measurement Method

Gravimetric (TGA), Breakthrough (packed bed), Volumetric (isotherm), or Other.

Gas Flow Rate

Volumetric flow rate (mL/min) during adsorption. Not applicable for isotherm measurements.

t₉₅

Time (min) for breakthrough column CO₂ outlet to reach 95% of inlet concentration. Used to calculate cycle time.

Column Diameter

Inner diameter (cm) of the breakthrough column.

Sorbent Mass / Cycle Time

Mass (g) in the column or TGA pan. Cycle time is calculated (not directly recorded) from standardized breakthrough parameters.

About this project

The Database

Compiled as part of a study on benchmarking strategies for solid DAC sorbents. A SciFinder search for "Direct Air Capture" yielded 1,242 publications as of October 2024. After screening, 163 papers totalling over 900 unique sorbent entries were included. This is a representative sample, not a complete catalog, and will be continuously updated.
