Standardized Testing & Reporting Guidelines

Key recommendations

📐 Standardized performance parameters

Report CO₂ capacity, adsorption–desorption rate, and degradation rate as the standard set of performance parameters. These are the most commonly analyzed metrics and can be measured with equipment already available in most labs.

🔁 Degradation testing

Degradation rate is reported for only ~20% of sorbents, yet can be measured using the same setup as CO₂ capacity by extending the number of cycles. We recommend testing ≥10 cycles minimum, under simulated air conditions.

⚗️ Reference materials

Include well-characterized reference materials (Lewatit VP OC 1065, ZIF-8, γ-Al₂O₃, Zeolite 13X) alongside new sorbents to validate equipment performance and ensure inter-laboratory comparability.

🌍 Deployment-relevant conditions

Test sorbents under a range of humidity (0–80% RH), temperature, and CO₂ partial pressure (0.35–0.45 mbar, corresponding to sea level to 2000 m altitude) to capture real-world variability at DAC deployment sites.

📊 Kinetics reporting

Include uptake-vs-time data along with all normalization parameters (gas flow rate, sorbent loading, CO₂ concentration, bed dimensions for PBR). TGA and PBR measure fundamentally different quantities and should not be compared directly.

🧪 Oxygen exposure

Sorbents tested only under inert gas during regeneration may report overestimated stabilities. Report degradation behavior under simulated air conditions during both adsorption and regeneration to provide realistic stability assessments.

Deployment-relevance (DR) score

The DR score quantifies how well a sorbent is characterized for technoeconomic analysis at a given DAC deployment site. Points are awarded for reporting CO₂ capacity (+5), isotherms (+3), kinetics (+5), degradation under cycling (+5), and additional tested conditions such as multiple humidity levels, temperatures, and CO₂ concentrations. Maximum score reflects a fully deployment-characterized sorbent.

About This Database

Methods, team, citation, and contribution information.

Overview

This database was compiled as part of a study on benchmarking strategies for solid direct air capture (DAC) sorbents. A representative sample of solid sorbent papers from DAC literature was identified via a SciFinder search using the keyword "Direct Air Capture," resulting in 1,242 publications. After screening for relevance and experimental CO₂ capacity data below 5,000 ppm CO₂, 163 papers totalling over 600 unique sorbent entries were included.

For each entry, sorbent properties and experimental parameters were recorded including sorbent name and type, CO₂ capacity, capture and release temperatures, CO₂ concentration, gas flow rate, humidity, adsorption kinetics, regeneration method, cycling stability, and degradation cause.

Sorbent Classification

Amine: All materials containing an amine functional group that interacts with CO₂, regardless of support type.

MOF: Crystalline metal–organic frameworks without amine groups participating in CO₂ binding.

Metal oxide: Inorganic metal–oxygen compounds.

Other: All remaining sorbent types (COFs, zeolites, ionic liquids, etc.).
